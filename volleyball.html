History

Volleyball, originally called “mintonette” was invented in 1895 by William G. Morgan after basketball was invented four years before. Morgan was a graduate of the Springfield College of the YMCA who designed a combination of basketball, baseball, tennis, and handball. The first volleyball net was only 6’6” high which was borrowed from tennis. In 1916 in the Philippines, the offensive style of setting and style was demonstrated. Over the following years, standard rules were being needed for tournament play which ended up with the USVBA (United States Volleyball Association) to be formed in 1928.

Timeline of Significant Volleyball Events

  • In 1900, the sport got a special ball that was designed for volleyball.
  • In 1916, the offensive style of passing the ball to be struck by another player were introduced (the set and spike).
  • In 1917, the game was changed from 21 to 15 points.
  • In 1920, three hits per side were allowed along with back row attack rules were instituted.
  • In 1922, the first YMCA national championships were held in Brooklyn, NY. There were twenty-seven teams from 11 states that were represented.
  • In 1928, the United States Volleyball Association was formed because it became clear that tournaments and rules were needed.
  • In 1930, the first beach volleyball game was played.
  • In 1934, national volleyball referees were approved and recognized.
  • In 1947, the Federation Internationale De Volley-Ball (FIVB) was founded.
  • In 1948, the first beach tournament was held.
  • In 1964, volleyball was introduced to the Olympic Games in Tokyo.
  • In 1986, the Women’s Professional Volleyball Association (WPVA) was formed.
  • In 1990, the World League was created.
  • In 1995, the sport of volleyball was 100 years old.
  • In 1996, two-person beach volleyball became an Olympic Sport.

Basic Volleyball Games

  • 6 players playing on each side (3 in the front and 3 in the back).
  • Maximum of 3 hits per side.
  • Player may not hit the ball twice in a row.
  • The ball may be played off the net during a volley and when you serve
  • A ball hitting the boundary line in “in”.
  • A ball is out if it hits the antennae, the floor outside the court, the ceiling, etc.
